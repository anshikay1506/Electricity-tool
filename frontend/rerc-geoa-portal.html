RERC GEOA Regulations 2025 — Reference

Rajasthan Electricity Regulatory Commission (Terms and Conditions for Green Energy Open Access) Regulations, 2025 · Petition No. 2264/2024

Key Regulations
Reg. 1 — Title & Scope Reg. 2 — Definitions Reg. 3 — Eligibility Reg. 4 — Categorization Reg. 5 — Application Process Reg. 7 — Nodal Agency Reg. 8 — Allotment Priority Reg. 11 — Charges Reg. 12 — Banking Reg. 16 — Scheduling Reg. 21 — Green Energy Tariff Reg. 23 — Green Certificate

Regulation 1 — Short Title, Commencement and Extent

These Regulations shall be called the Rajasthan Electricity Regulatory Commission (Terms and Conditions for Green Energy Open Access) Regulations, 2025.

These Regulations apply to the whole state of Rajasthan and govern Open Access to electricity generated from Renewable Energy Sources, both captive and third-party, for use of Intra-State Transmission System (InSTS) and/or distribution systems of licensees.

These Regulations shall prevail over any other Commission regulation in force for allowing Open Access to green energy. They come into force from the date the Commission notifies.

Regulation 2 — Key Definitions

  • Banking: Surplus green energy injected into the grid and credited with the distribution licensee by GEOA consumers, to be drawn along with applicable charges to compensate additional costs.
  • Green Energy Open Access Consumer (GEOA Consumer): Any person with contract demand/sanctioned load of 100 kW or more (single or aggregated across multiple connections in the same electricity division) eligible to take green energy through open access.
  • Nodal Agency: RVPN for InSTS access; Distribution Licensee of the area for access within distribution system or for intra-division consumers.
  • Open Access: Non-discriminatory provision for use of transmission lines or distribution systems or associated facilities with such lines or systems by any licensee or consumer.
  • Obligated Entity: Entities mandated under Section 86(1)(e) of the Electricity Act, 2003 to fulfil the Renewable Purchase Obligation (RPO).
  • BESS: Battery Energy Storage System used for storage and supply of electricity.

Regulation 3 — Eligibility

The following persons/entities are eligible for Green Energy Open Access:

  • Any person with contract demand or sanctioned load of 100 kW or more, either through a single connection or through multiple connections aggregating 100 kW or more, located in the same electricity division of a distribution licensee.
  • Captive consumers have no lower load limit — they may apply for GEOA regardless of load size.
  • Consumers may elect to purchase green energy either up to a certain percentage of their consumption or for their entire consumption.
  • Any consumer may requisition for green energy from their distribution licensee for solar and/or non-solar Green Energy separately.
Green energy purchased from distribution licensee or from Renewable Energy sources in excess of the Renewable Purchase Obligation (RPO) of the obligated entity, if not utilized by them, shall be counted towards RPO compliance of the distribution licensee.

Regulation 4 — Categorization of GEOA

GEOA consumers are classified based on the duration of use of the intra-state transmission and/or distribution system:

  • Long-term GEOA: Duration of 12 years to 25 years
  • Medium-term GEOA: Duration of 1 year to 12 years
  • Short-term GEOA: Duration up to 1 year (minimum 1 month)

For consumers sourcing power through a distribution licensee under the Green Energy Tariff, the minimum requisition period is one year and the quantum of green energy must be pre-specified for at least one year.

Regulation 5 — Application Process

An application for Green Energy Open Access shall be submitted to the Nodal Agency with the following:

  • Details of the applicant — name, address, consumer number
  • Nature and quantum of open access required (capacity in kW/MW)
  • Period for which open access is sought and proposed start date
  • Source/point of injection and drawal
  • Voltage level of connection
  • Name of the green energy generator / RE developer
  • Nature of energy sourcing (captive, third-party, power exchange, Green Energy Tariff)
  • Details of BESS if applicable
The application is to be submitted on a one-time basis for long-term and medium-term access. Short-term access applications may be submitted as required for each period.

Regulation 7 — Role of Nodal Agency

The Nodal Agency (RVPN or Distribution Licensee) shall:

  • Receive applications from GEOA consumers and check completeness
  • Forward the application to SLDC for technical feasibility check
  • Communicate SLDC's decision to the applicant within 30 days of receipt of complete application
  • Issue the Open Access order and facilitate execution of agreements
  • Maintain a register of all GEOA consumers

SLDC shall check the availability of spare transmission/distribution capacity and furnish its report to the Nodal Agency within the stipulated time.

Regulation 8 — Allotment Priority

Priority for grant of open access shall be as follows:

  • 1st Priority: Distribution Licensees (highest priority)
  • 2nd Priority: GEOA consumers over normal Open Access consumers
  • 3rd Priority: Long-term GEOA consumers over Medium-term, which takes priority over Short-term, at any given time, subject to availability of spare T&D capacity margins.
The decision to allow green energy open access shall be based on a first-come, first-served basis within the same priority category.

Regulation 11 — Charges Applicable to GEOA Consumers

The following charges apply to Green Energy Open Access consumers:

  • 11.1 Transmission Charges: Standard InSTS charges applicable. Solar/Wind plants (with/without BESS >5% of RE capacity) are exempt from 100% T&W charges for 7 years from COD.
  • 11.2 BESS Incentives: Standalone BESS exempt from 100% T&W charges on power supplied during peak/non-solar hours for 7 years. BESS connected at 11kV/33kV sub-stations exempt from 100% T&W charges.
  • 11.3 Cross-Subsidy Surcharge (CSS): Applicable to non-captive GEOA consumers as determined by RERC. Partial exemptions may apply for qualifying green energy categories.
  • 11.4 Additional Surcharge: May be levied on GEOA consumers where the fixed charge gap exists. Shall not exceed the per-unit fixed cost of power purchase of the distribution licensee concerned. To be phased out within 4 years as per Electricity (Amendment) Rules, 2024.
  • 11.5 SLDC/Scheduling Charges: As determined by RERC from time to time.
  • 11.6 Standby Charges: 25% of applicable energy charges when GEOA consumer draws from Discom due to generator outage. Not applicable if 1-day advance notice is given.
  • 11.7 Green Hydrogen/Ammonia Plants: 50% waiver of InSTS T&W charges for solar/wind plants (with/without storage) supplying Green Hydrogen/Ammonia plants set up by 2030.

Regulation 12 — Banking of Green Energy

Banking of surplus green energy is permitted for GEOA consumers under the following terms:

  • Surplus green energy injected by a GEOA consumer is credited to their account with the distribution licensee
  • The banked energy may be drawn by the consumer along with applicable banking charges
  • Banking charges are determined by the Commission and may include compensation for additional costs incurred by the licensee
  • Banking is permitted on a monthly basis; unutilized banked energy at month-end may lapse or be settled as per Commission orders
Green energy in excess of Renewable Purchase Obligation (RPO) of the obligated entity — if not utilized by them in any manner — shall be counted towards RPO compliance of the distribution licensee.

Regulation 16 — Scheduling of Power

GEOA consumers must comply with SLDC scheduling requirements:

  • Day-ahead scheduling of injection and drawal must be submitted to SLDC as per prescribed format
  • Deviations from schedule are subject to deviation settlement mechanism (DSM) charges
  • GEOA consumer failing to utilize allocated capacity more than 4 times in a month (each under-utilization exceeding 4 hours) may have their open access cancelled after due notice
  • Surplus capacity arising from surrendered or cancelled short-term access may be reallocated to other short-term consumers in order of pending applications

Regulation 21 — Green Energy Tariff

Consumers may procure green energy through their distribution licensee under the Green Energy Tariff route:

  • Consumer places a requisition with the distribution licensee for the desired quantum of green energy
  • Minimum requisition period: one year
  • The quantum of green energy must be pre-specified for at least one year
  • Consumer may give separate requisition for solar and non-solar Green Energy
  • The distribution licensee shall procure such quantity of green energy and supply it to the consumer
Eligibility criteria for Green Energy Open Access Power Purchases and Green Power Purchases through the respective Distribution Licensee through the Green Power Tariff may be defined separately under this regulation.

Regulation 23 — Green Certificate

The distribution licensee shall issue a Green Certificate on a yearly basis to consumers for the green energy supplied:

  • Certificate is issued for green energy supplied by the licensee to the consumer on their request
  • Certificate is issued for green energy beyond the Renewable Purchase Obligation (RPO) of the consumer
  • The certificate enables entities to fulfil their commitments such as RE-100, Net-Zero goals, and global SBTi standards
  • The base value, validity period, and redemption process of the Green Certificate shall be well-defined and follow Commission guidelines
